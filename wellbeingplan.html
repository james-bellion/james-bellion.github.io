Wellbeing Plan

My wellbeing plan using Tā Mason Duries Te Whare Tapa Whā model.

  • Taha Tinana (Physical Wellbeing)
  • Taha Whānau (Family Wellbeing)
  • Taha Hinengaro (Emotional and Mental Wellbeing)
  • Taha Wairua (Spiritual Wellbeing)
  • Whenua (Interconnections to the land and environment)

Physical Wellbeing

To help maintain my Physical Wellbeing I Have made time throughout the week for regular exercise, including family walks at the weekend up mount kao kao and our local walking tracks close to Home

Family Wellbeing

To help maintain my Family Wellbeing I cook and eat with my family every night and have a chance to sit down and conect after our busy days.

Emotional and Mental Wellbeing

To help maintain my Emotional and Mental Wellbeing I have made an effort to pratice 5 min mindfullness meditation each day. And to have positive self reflection after each day, really making an effort to adopt a groth Mindset.

I am also making a effort to catch and frame any negative self talk that I notice and trun my mindset into a positive one, seeking more optimisium.

Spiritual Wellbeing

To Improve my Spiritual Wellbeing every morning on the way into the city I ask myself three things I am thankful for each day. This can often lead to ariving to the course in a positive mindset

Interconnections to the land and environment

As a lover of nature I really enjoy this one. I have found this more dificult throughout the course as the winter is setting in and the work load is very heavy but when ever I can I always enjoy taking a walk into the local bushwalks and hearing the sound of the rivers and all the green. It can have a great effect on my wellbeing and When i do this at the end of the week often taking my 3 year old son with me I feel its a great way to reset and connect to nature. I try do do this ever week with Otari wilton bushwalk being my current top spot.

Camping Is one of my passions, solo or with groups. I find getting out in the middle of the moutains, forrests and lakes is my faveriote way to connect with the land. Bonus to have a dip in the Rivers!
