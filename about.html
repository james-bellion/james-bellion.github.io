Explain a situation where you have made an ethical decision. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made, and how you reflect on the decision now.

A. I am a father of Two, my wonderful children are Axel and Mila. I was 25 years old when my amazing partner became pregnant with our first son Axel. It was an unplanned pregnancy and at the time being 25 I still considered myself a young parent to be, even Tho thinking about It many people have children all over the world at a much younger age but for personal context I was “the first one” to have children out of friends my own age.

I am happy we agreed to go ahead with the pregnancy opposed to other options and having children has given me a great sense of fulfillment and happiness. I believe it has also changed me to become a better person.

Share an example of when you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss strategies you tried at the time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now and why.

For context I used to work as a chef and was proud of my achievements that led me to a leadership role where I never pictured myself in at the time as a lowly dishwasher and I learnt a lot about life in this role and enjoyed the job!

Kitchen environments can be very hostile places and a high-pressure environment, especially on busy days.

One day I was in charge the kitchen, calling out the orders, coordinating with other chefs, restaurant manager, sending out the food, always communicating ect as chefs do.

One of the chefs was having an off day and was making mistakes with food orders. I have seen many chefs get angry in these situations and would shout at the poor guy and it just makes the situation ugly and worse. I could feel the pressure as some tickets where mounting up and we were falling behind but I remembered the chilled out, older, very experienced guy Connor who used to lead our team at one time and how he just slowed things down and took a look at what was going wrong and methodically fixed the problem by talking to the team and calmly giving out instructions to get us back on track and that’s exactly what I did and feel this approach got me out of this tricky situation in no time and we were back on track.

I just kept communicating with the chef who was making the mistakes as he was feeling frustrated and angry, but I kept encouraging him whilst giving him advice on what to do and when to do it. It can be a lot of multi-tasking but kept the team together and we all put in a good effort.

These situations happen, they have happened to me, so I knew how it’s stressful. It’s positive to help others grow. I may not have realized it at that moment in time, but it helped me grow as a leader.
