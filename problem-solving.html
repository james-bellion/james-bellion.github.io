My experiance with blocks

This week I was stuck on the unjumble test. The objective was to complete the unjumble function, which accepts a string as an argument and reverses it, then returns the reversed string, using the built-in JavaScript methods for strings and arrays to accomplish this.

I first tried to break it down in steps using pseudo code and figure out what I needed to do but I was not using the correct methods and was not able to find the answer right away!

It was frustrating to say the least, so I took a break and went and had lunch. I learnt it was imported not to get to worked up about it.

"Its not a failed task, it just hasn’t been solved yet."

Wroking Through the problem

One cheese toastie later I came back to the problem with fresh eyes and researched the methods I needed using google. I then planned to tackle the issue by writing some pseudo code, step by step in the order of what I needed to do. I then ran the code with a console.log and it worked !

It felt great having solved the task in contrast to feeling frustrated earlier. It was also a relief to complete the task and move on to the next.

Console logging to check the results

Reflections on how confident you feel using each of these problem-solving techniques/processes:

Pseudocode:

Depending how complex the task is I feel confident with this process as long as I know the right order in which to write my code.

Trying something:

Trial and error is great and I am semi confident trying different ways of writing the code.

Rubber ducky method:

I feel like I am getting lots of practice using this technique and feel confident with it. I find it help me learn a lot as well as picking out those pesky character errors.

Reading error messages:

I still feel as if I am getting used to this method but it’s very helpful to check as it can point you towards the solution. Or gives you something to research to find the answer.

Console.logging

Sometimes being the only way to check your code console logging is becoming a great weapon in my problem-solving arsenal. I’m feeling more confident using I as I go through my code but there is still room for improvement!

Googling:

One I am most familiar with. I don’t know what I would do without it sometime! very confident in finding examples of techniques to use through google giving me a greater understanding of the problem at hand.

Asking your peers for help:

Great benefit of the forums and home groups is having this option. I have asked for help on the forums, and I have had great feedback so far.

Asking coaches for help:

Not shy to send my facilitators a message when I’m stuck. I think its important to ask them for help when I get really stuck on a certain task and I’m grateful for the help they provide that keeps me from getting stuck in the mud.

Improving your process with reflection:

I personally use this a lot to try and improve my process. Just taking a couple of minutes to go over what I have just learnt helps me retain the knowledge.

Reflect on a time you were reluctant to ask for help. Consider what made you reluctant to do so.

During the first intro into JavaScript, I failed a few more of the tests then I would have liked, and I felt reluctant to ask for help. Instead, I did lots of extra exercises to help build my knowledge and coding muscle memory so I could come back and tackle the challenge again.

If I was to still have issues going forward from there, I would have made the decision to reach out, but luckily the extra resources I worked through boosted my understanding.
